The Moat
Why first-mover wins forever

Historical Data Advantage

The longer Topology runs, the richer the relationship timeline. A new entrant sees a snapshot; Topology users see years of evolution, patterns, and drift that can't be recreated.

Graph Complexity Moat

Social graphs are incredibly personal. Rebuilding one from scratch is months of data collection. Nobody switches — the cost is starting from zero on your most intimate data.

Cross-Platform Lock-in

Topology aggregates from iMessage, WhatsApp, Slack, Instagram DMs, and more. The more sources connected, the truer the picture — and the harder to replicate.

Virality
Goes viral because...
01

The graph is irresistible to screenshot

"Here's what my actual social universe looks like" — the visualization alone gets shared millions of times. Everyone wants to see their shape.

02

Asymmetric revelations spark conversations

"I found out I initiate 90% of our conversations" becomes the text that changes a friendship. These insights are inherently conversational.

03

Bridge connector status is social currency

Discovering you're the critical link between friend groups is deeply flattering. "Topology says I'm a Bridge Connector" becomes a flex. People tag their bridges.

Features
Everything Topology does
🌐

Living Graph

Real-time network visualization that evolves as your relationships change. Pinch, zoom, explore your social universe.

📉

Drift Detection

Automatic alerts when important relationships are fading before you consciously notice.

⚖️

Reciprocity Analysis

See who initiates, who responds, who's investing equally, and who isn't — across all platforms.

🌉

Bridge Mapping

Identify the people who hold your social world together. Critical connectors highlighted and tracked.

🏘️

Cluster Discovery

Auto-detect friend groups, work circles, family clusters. See overlaps and isolation zones.

📅

Relationship Timeline

Scroll back through any relationship's history — see how communication ebbed and flowed over months and years.

🔒

Zero-Knowledge Privacy

All graph computation runs on-device. Communication content is never read — only metadata patterns.
